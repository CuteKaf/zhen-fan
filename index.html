About Me

I am a Master's student at NUS and a Digitalization Engineer. My research focuses on bridging the gap between virtual simulation and physical reality to build robust Embodied AI systems.

Technically, I specialize in NVIDIA Omniverse (Isaac Sim) and High-Fidelity Digital Twin Modeling. I have developed a self-built Automated Synthetic Data Pipeline—incorporating COSMOS (Text-to-Video) generation—to train VLM and Action Recognition models. My proposed Sim-to-Real pipeline has achieved 90.31% accuracy in real-world validation, proving the effectiveness of synthetic training.

My long-term goal is to apply these simulation capabilities to Human-Robot Collaboration (HRC). By solving the "data scarcity" problem with synthetic data, I aim to enable robots to learn complex, safe, and interactive behaviors before they ever touch the real world.

  • Simulation & Digital Twin: NVIDIA Omniverse (Isaac Sim), High-Fidelity Modeling, Asset Binding.
  • Synthetic Data Pipeline: Text-to-Video (COSMOS), Automated Generation, Domain Randomization.
  • Sim-to-Real Transfer: Action Recognition (90.31% Accuracy), VLM Training, Reality Gap Reduction.
